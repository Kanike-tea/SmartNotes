2-6. EULER GRAPHS

As mentioned in Chapter 1, graph theory was born in 1736 with Euler’s
famous paper in which he solved the Kénigsberg bridge problem. In the same
paper, Euler posed (and then solved) a more general problem: In what type of
graph G is it possible to find a closed walk running through every edge of G
