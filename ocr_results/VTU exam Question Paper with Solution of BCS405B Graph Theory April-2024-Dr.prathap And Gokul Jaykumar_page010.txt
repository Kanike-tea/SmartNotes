Edge-connectivity
Let G be a connected graph and S be a cut-set in G that contains fewest number of edge:
The number of edges in S is called the edge-connectivity (or the line connectivity) of G. In
other words, the edge-connectivity of G is the minimum number of edges of G whose remov:

results in a disconnected or trivial graph. Obviously, the edge-connectivity of G is at least one,
It is trivial to note that in a tree every edge (branch) is a cut-set. Therefore, the edge-

connectivity of a tree is one.
For the graph shown in Figure 4.36 no single edge is a cut-set. But the graph has a cut-set
{e2, e3) that contains two edges. Therefore, the edge-connectivity of this graph is two.
